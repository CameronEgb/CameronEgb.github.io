Dr. Evelyn Reed

Associate Professor of Computational Linguistics

I am a researcher and educator at the University of Techwood, specializing in Natural Language Processing (NLP) and how computational models can understand and generate human language. My work focuses on developing more efficient and interpretable AI systems for complex language tasks.

I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Stanford University and was a postdoctoral researcher at MIT's CSAIL. I am passionate about mentoring the next generation of AI researchers and fostering a collaborative and inclusive research environment.

Research Interests

Model Interpretability

Developing techniques to understand the "black box" of neural networks, making their decisions more transparent and trustworthy.

Low-Resource NLP

Creating effective NLP models for languages with limited data, aiming to make language technology more equitable.

Commonsense Reasoning

Equipping AI with the implicit, everyday knowledge humans use to understand the world and communicate fluently.
